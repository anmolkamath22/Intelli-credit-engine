Detailed Rationale

Crisil Ratings has reaffirmed its ‘Crisil A1+’ rating on the commercial paper programme of Blue Star Ltd (BSL).

 

The rating continues to reflect the strong business risk profile of BSL, driven by healthy diversity in revenue from electromechanical projects and commercial air conditioning (EMP) and unitary products (UP) segments. The rating factors in the strong market position of BSL in the EMP segment, with healthy order book and gradually increasing market share in the UP segment. The financial risk profile is strong, marked by high networth and strong capital structure. These strengths are partially offset by large working capital requirement and susceptibility to cyclicality in demand from end-user industries.

 

The operating income has grown moderately by ~4% (year-on-year [y-o-y]) in the first quarter of fiscal 2026. While the EMP segment has grown ~36% (y-o-y), the UP segment declined ~13%, due to impacted sales of room air conditioners (RACs) due to early monsoon. Healthy diversification has minimised the weather impact on the overall growth of the company. The goods and services tax rationalisation in air conditioners coupled with the festive season may boost demand in the second half of this fiscal. The margin moderated to 6.7% in the first quarter of fiscal 2026, from the exceptionally high ~8.3% in the corresponding period of the previous fiscal, due to operating deleverage in the UP segment and project and product mix-related changes in the EMP segment. The company maintained a strong order book of Rs 6,843 crore as of June 2025, majorly from the EMP segment.

 

In fiscal 2025, the operating income increased ~24% (y-o-y) to Rs 11,938 crore, led by growth of ~22% in the UP segment and ~27% in the EMP segment. Revenue growth in the UP segment (majorly RACs) is driven by the strong summer season demand and healthy festive sales, supported by consumer finance schemes. The EMP segment witnessed strong order finalisations, primarily from factories and the data center market segment. The operating margin improved to 7.1% in fiscal 2025, from 6% in fiscal 2024, led by operating leverage and the cost management program.

 

The financial risk profile is marked by strong networth of ~Rs 2,904 crore and low gearing of 0.1 time as on March 31, 2025, supported by steady growth in cash accrual. Debt protection metrics remain strong, with interest coverage ratio of ~18 times in fiscal 2025; the metrics are expected to remain healthy over the medium term. Liquidity remains strong, with unencumbered cash and cash equivalents of Rs 843 crore as on March 31, 2025. Further, the fund-based bank limit (Rs 665 crore) was utilised at below 10% on average during the 12 months through March 2025.

Analytical Approach

Crisil Ratings has consolidated the business and financial risk profiles of BSL and its subsidiaries -- Blue Star Engineering and Electronics Ltd, Blue Star Qatar WLL, Blue Star International FZCO, Blue MEA Airconditioning LLC, BSL AC&R (Singapore) Pte Ltd, Blue Star Climatech Ltd, Blue Star North America Inc, Blue Star Europe BV, Blue Star Innovation Japan LLC, Blue Star Air Conditioning & Refrigeration (U) Ltd, Blue Star Oman Electro-Mechanical Co LLC (joint venture [JV]) and Blue Star M&E Engineering (Sdn) Bhd (JV) due to the similar nature of their operations.

Key Rating Drivers - Strengths

Diversified revenue profile

Presence in industrial (EMP) and consumer (UP) segments mitigates the risk of slowdown in any one segment or industry. BSL relies almost equally on both these segments, in terms of revenue and profitability. The UP segment contributed around 47% of revenue in fiscal 2025, with earnings before interest and taxes (Ebit) margin of around 8.4%; contribution of the EMP segment was higher at 50% with Ebit margin of 8.2%.

 

Healthy market position across business segments

BSL has an established track record and strong market position in the EMP segment and commercial air conditioner business. The company is also the largest player in the commercial refrigeration business in the UP segment and has increased its market share in the RAC business to 14% in fiscal 2025, from 10% in fiscal 2016. Its widespread distribution network -- of over 4,000 channel partners and 10,000 outlets across the country -- supports the strong market position.

 

Strong financial risk profile

Financial risk profile is marked by strong networth of ~Rs 2,904 crore and low gearing of 0.1 time as on March 31, 2025, supported by steady growth in cash accrual. Debt remains low at ~Rs 199 crore as on March 31, 2025, majorly for the working capital requirement. Debt protection metrics remain strong, with interest coverage ratio of ~18 times in fiscal 2025; the metrics are expected to remain healthy over the medium term.

 

The company has planned capital expenditure (capex) for capacity expansion apart from regular maintenance capex, which is expected to be majorly funded by internal cash accrual. Debt protections metrics are expected to remain strong over the medium term.

Key Rating Drivers - Weaknesses

Susceptibility to downturns in end-user industries

Demand for the EMP segment depends on capex in end-user industries, which is further corelated with the macroeconomic environment. Consequently, the amount of capex reduces during economic downturn, leading to lower order inflow impacting operating performance.

 

Working capital intensive operations

Operations are working capital intensive, given the engineering, procurement and construction nature of operations in the EMP segment. Gross current assets were around 176 days as on March 31, 2025. However, majority of the working capital is financed through advances from customers and payables, thus mitigating the risk.

Liquidity: Strong

Liquidity has been healthy, as indicated by unencumbered cash and cash equivalents of Rs 843 crore as on March 31, 2025. The fund-based facility of Rs 665 crore was utilised at below 10% on average during the 12 months through March 31, 2025. Cash accrual of Rs 550-600 crore per annum is expected to be sufficient to meet capex as well as incremental working capital requirement.

 

ESG profile

The environment, social and governance (ESG) profile of BSL supports its already strong credit risk profile. The sector has moderate environmental and social impact, driven by its raw material sourcing strategies, waste-intensive processes and direct impact on the health and wellbeing of customers.

 

Key ESG highlights

  • BSL’s share of renewables in total energy consumption improved to ~10% in fiscal 2025, from ~9% in fiscal 2024.
  • All its manufacturing sites are equipped with rainwater harvesting systems and zero liquid discharge site, supported by advanced effluent treatment plants and sewage treatment plants.
  • The manufacturing facilities in Wada (Maharashtra) are certified with Indian Green Building Council (IGBC) Platinum Rating, while the Dadra (Dadra and Nagar Haveli) and Sri City (Andhra Pradesh) plants have been certified with IGBC Gold Rating under the category of Green Factory Building.
  • The share of female employees (permanent and other than permanent) increased to 10.4% in fiscal 2025, from ~9% in fiscal 2024. Further, the attrition rate of permanent employees reduced to ~15% in fiscal 2025 (as compared to ~16% in fiscal 2024).
  • The governance structure is characterised by ~43% of its board being independent directors, ~15% women board directors and extensive financial disclosures.

 

ESG is gaining importance among investors and lenders. The commitment of BSL to ESG will play a key role in enhancing stakeholder confidence, given the shareholding by foreign portfolio investors and access to both domestic and foreign capital markets.

Rating sensitivity factors

Downward factors

  • Sustained decline in operating performance, leading to return on capital employed ratio to below 15% on a sustainable basis
  • Any large, debt-funded capex, leading to weakening of the financial risk profile

About the Company

BSL was established in 1943 by late Mr Mohan T Advani. It is India’s leading central air-conditioning and commercial refrigeration company. Its manufacturing facilities are spread across India including Ahmedabad, Dadra, Thane, Himachal Pradesh and Sri City. Operations can be classified into three segments -- EMP and packaged air conditioning systems, UP and professional electronics and industrial systems business -- which contributed 50%, 47% and 3%, respectively, to the consolidated sales in fiscal 2025. It operates in more than 18 international markets in the Middle East, Africa and South Asia. BSL has started new subsidiaries in North America and Europe to further expand its international business.
